# Madrid, Spain Complete Travel Guide

## Overview
Madrid, Spain's vibrant capital and largest city, sits in the heart of the Iberian Peninsula at 2,100 feet above sea level. This cosmopolitan metropolis seamlessly blends royal heritage with contemporary culture, offering world-class museums, magnificent architecture, legendary nightlife, and some of Europe's finest cuisine. Known for its late dining culture and energetic atmosphere, Madrid is a city that truly comes alive after dark.

## Best Time to Visit
- **Peak Season (June-August)**: Hot, sunny days with temperatures reaching 95°F. Tourist crowds and higher prices.
- **Shoulder Season (April-May, September-October)**: Perfect weather with mild temperatures 65-75°F, fewer crowds.
- **Winter (December-February)**: Cool and crisp, occasional rain. Average highs 50-55°F. Fewer tourists, lower prices.
- **Spring (March-May)**: Pleasant temperatures, blooming parks, ideal for walking and outdoor dining.

**Best Months**: April-May and September-October for perfect weather and cultural events.

## Getting There & Around

### Airports
- **Adolfo Suárez Madrid-Barajas Airport (MAD)**: Main international airport, 9 miles northeast of city center
- **Madrid-Cuatro Vientos Airport (LECU)**: Small regional airport primarily for private flights

### Transportation
- **Metro de Madrid**: Excellent subway system with 12 lines. Single ticket €1.50, 10-trip pass €12.20
- **Cercanías**: Regional train network connecting suburbs and nearby cities like Toledo
- **EMT Bus Network**: Comprehensive city bus system, same pricing as metro
- **Taxi**: White cars with red diagonal stripe, starting fare €2.50, airport supplement €5.50
- **BiciMAD**: Electric bike sharing system throughout city center
- **Walking**: Historic center highly walkable, most attractions within walking distance

## Neighborhoods & Areas

### Sol-Gran Vía (City Center)
The beating heart of Madrid with major shopping, theaters, and iconic landmarks.
- **Attractions**: Puerta del Sol, Plaza Mayor, Royal Palace, Gran Vía theaters
- **Shopping**: El Corte Inglés, Fnac, international brands along Gran Vía
- **Best For**: First-time visitors, theater lovers, central location

### La Latina
Historic neighborhood famous for tapas bars, Sunday flea market, and medieval streets.
- **Highlights**: El Rastro flea market, traditional taverns, Plaza de la Paja
- **Dining**: Casa Lucas, Taberna Los Huevos de Lucio, El Viajero rooftop
- **Best For**: Food lovers, authentic Spanish experience, weekend exploring

### Malasaña
Trendy, alternative neighborhood with vintage shops, indie bars, and countercultural vibe.
- **Attractions**: Plaza del Dos de Mayo, vintage clothing stores, record shops
- **Nightlife**: Tupperware Club, Café Central jazz club, numerous cocktail bars
- **Best For**: Young travelers, nightlife enthusiasts, alternative culture

### Chueca
LGBTQ+-friendly district known for designer boutiques, gourmet restaurants, and vibrant nightlife.
- **Shopping**: Independent designer stores, vintage boutiques, specialty food shops
- **Dining**: Bazaar, La Carmencita, Honest Greens
- **Best For**: LGBTQ+ travelers, fashion enthusiasts, upscale dining

### Salamanca
Upscale district with luxury shopping, elegant architecture, and high-end restaurants.
- **Shopping**: Calle Serrano (luxury boutiques), ABC Serrano shopping center
- **Dining**: Ramón Freixa Madrid (Michelin starred), José Andrés restaurants
- **Best For**: Luxury travelers, high-end shopping, fine dining

### Retiro
Elegant residential area surrounding the famous park, with museums and cultural sites.
- **Attractions**: Retiro Park, Prado Museum, Reina Sofía Museum
- **Dining**: Café del Jardín, Florida Retiro, Estado Puro
- **Best For**: Art lovers, families, peaceful atmosphere

## Top Attractions

### World-Class Museums
1. **Prado Museum**: One of world's finest art museums featuring Velázquez, Goya, El Greco. Open Mon-Sat 10am-8pm, Sun 10am-7pm. Adults €15, students €7.50.

2. **Reina Sofía Museum**: Modern and contemporary art including Picasso's Guernica. Open Mon, Wed-Sat 10am-9pm, Sun 10am-7pm. Adults €12, students €6.

3. **Thyssen-Bornemisza Museum**: Completes Madrid's "Golden Triangle" with impressive collection spanning 8 centuries. Open Tue-Sun 10am-7pm. Adults €13.

4. **Royal Palace (Palacio Real)**: Official residence of Spanish Royal Family with 3,418 rooms, only used for ceremonies. Open daily 10am-8pm (Oct-Mar until 6pm). Adults €13.

5. **Archaeological Museum**: Extensive collection of Spanish artifacts from prehistoric to modern times. Open Tue-Sat 9:30am-8pm, Sun 9:30am-3pm. Adults €3.

### Historic Landmarks
- **Plaza Mayor**: Grand rectangular square surrounded by red buildings with 237 balconies, heart of Habsburg Madrid
- **Puerta del Sol**: Central square and exact center of Spain, famous clock tower for New Year celebrations
- **Gran Vía**: Madrid's Broadway with theaters, shops, and impressive early 20th-century architecture
- **Plaza de Cibeles**: Iconic fountain and palace, symbol of Madrid, beautiful at night
- **Templo de Debod**: Authentic Egyptian temple gifted to Spain, surrounded by park with city views

### Parks
- **Retiro Park**: 350-acre central park with Crystal Palace, rose garden, and puppet shows on weekends
- **Casa de Campo**: Largest park in Madrid with cable car, zoo, amusement park, and great city views
- **Madrid Río**: Linear park along Manzanares River with walking paths, playgrounds, and cultural spaces

## Food & Drink Culture

### Essential Spanish Dishes
- **Jamón Ibérico**: Premium cured ham, considered Spain's finest delicacy
- **Cocido Madrileño**: Traditional chickpea stew with meat and vegetables, served in three courses
- **Calamares Sandwich**: Fried squid rings in crusty bread, Madrid street food staple
- **Tortilla Española**: Spanish potato omelet, found in every bar and restaurant
- **Churros con Chocolate**: Fried dough pastries with thick hot chocolate for dipping

## Practical Information

### Language & Communication
- **Official Language**: Spanish (Castilian)
- **English**: Widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, restaurants
- **Basic Phrases**: "Hola" (Hello), "Gracias" (Thank you), "Por favor" (Please), "¿Habla inglés?" (Do you speak English?)
- **Tipping**: 5-10% at restaurants, round up taxi fares, €1-2 for hotel staff

### Money & Costs
- **Currency**: Euro (€)
- **Budget Travelers**: €50-80/day (hostels, casual dining, public transport, free museums)
- **Mid-Range**: €120-200/day (3-star hotels, restaurant meals, paid attractions, taxis)
- **Luxury**: €300+/day (luxury hotels, fine dining, private tours, shopping)
- **ATMs**: Widely available, most accept international cards
- **Cards**: Credit cards accepted everywhere, contactless payment common

### Cultural Etiquette
- **Greeting**: Handshake or air kiss on both cheeks for friends
- **Dining**: Wait to be seated, keep hands visible on table, finish everything on your plate
- **Dress Code**: Smart casual for restaurants, more formal for upscale venues and opera
- **Noise Level**: Spaniards tend to speak loudly, especially during social gatherings
- **Personal Space**: Closer than Northern Europeans but respectful

### Climate & Clothing
- **Continental Climate**: Hot summers, cool winters, low humidity
- **Summer**: Light, breathable clothing, sun hat, comfortable walking shoes, evening layer
- **Winter**: Warm coat, layers, waterproof jacket for occasional rain
- **Spring/Fall**: Layers essential, light jacket, comfortable shoes for walking
- **Year-Round**: Sunglasses, comfortable walking shoes, small backpack for day trips

### Safety & Health
- **Safety**: Very safe city, standard precautions against pickpockets in tourist areas
- **Emergency Number**: 112 (police, fire, medical)
- **Healthcare**: Excellent public healthcare system, European Health Insurance Card accepted for EU citizens
- **Pharmacies**: Green cross sign, knowledgeable pharmacists, many open 24 hours
- **Water**: Tap water safe to drink throughout the city

### Time & Communication
- **Time Zone**: Central European Time (UTC+1, UTC+2 in summer)
- **Business Hours**: Generally 9am-2pm and 5pm-8pm Monday-Friday
- **Siesta**: Many shops close 2pm-5pm, restaurants close between lunch and dinner
- **Internet**: Free WiFi widely available in hotels, cafés, public spaces
- **Phone**: Good mobile coverage, international roaming available

## Seasonal Highlights

### Spring (March-May)
- **Weather**: Mild temperatures, blooming trees in parks
- **Events**: Easter processions, Feria del Libro (Book Fair)
- **Advantages**: Perfect weather for walking, outdoor dining, fewer crowds
- **Pack**: Light layers, light jacket, comfortable walking shoes

### Summer (June-August)
- **Weather**: Hot and sunny, minimal rainfall
- **Events**: Veranos de la Villa outdoor festival, Madrid Pride (early July)
- **Advantages**: Long daylight hours, vibrant street life, outdoor concerts
- **Considerations**: High temperatures, tourist crowds, higher prices

### Fall (September-November)
- **Weather**: Crisp and clear, beautiful autumn colors
- **Events**: Festival de Otoño, Noche en Blanco
- **Advantages**: Perfect weather, cultural season begins, fewer tourists
- **Pack**: Layers, light jacket, rain jacket just in case

### Winter (December-February)
- **Weather**: Cool and crisp, occasional rain
- **Events**: Christmas markets, Three Kings celebration (January 6)
- **Advantages**: Lower prices, cozy indoor atmosphere, holiday decorations
- **Considerations**: Shorter days, some outdoor attractions less appealing

### Local Insider Tips
1. **Free Museum Hours**: Many museums free certain hours/days - Prado free daily 6-8pm
2. **Siesta Respect**: Plan shopping and business around siesta hours (2-5pm)
3. **Late Dining**: Restaurants don't serve dinner until 8:30pm at earliest, locals eat at 10pm+
4. **Metro Efficiency**: Fastest way around the city, buy 10-trip ticket for savings
5. **Sunday Traditions**: El Rastro market morning, vermouth and tapas afternoon
6. **Dress Standards**: Madrilenos dress well, avoid athletic wear outside of exercise
7. **Language Effort**: Even basic Spanish appreciated, locals often helpful with directions
8. **Cash for Small Places**: Carry cash for small bars, markets, traditional establishments
9. **Booking Ahead**: Reserve tables for dinner, especially weekends and popular restaurants
10. **Explore by District**: Each neighborhood has distinct character, spend time in 2-3 per visit

### Special Interest Recommendations

**Art Lovers**: Focus on museum triangle, add contemporary galleries, art book stores
**Food Enthusiasts**: Take cooking class, visit multiple markets, try regional specialties
**History Buffs**: Include Habsburg Madrid walking tour, Royal sites day trips
**Nightlife Seekers**: Start with vermouth culture, progress through neighborhoods, end at clubs
**Families**: Retiro Park activities, kid-friendly museums, early dining times
**Luxury Travelers**: Stay in Salamanca, high-end shopping, Michelin-starred dining experiences
